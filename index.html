The loop

Touch. Light. Haptics. Voice.

No typing. No notifications. Three ways to say I’m thinking of you — each one a small ritual, not a message in a noisy channel.

Echo

Hold a friend’s bead and speak. A short voice charm — their real voice, not a highlight reel — lands on your wrist, glows, and waits to be replayed.

Pulse

Tap a bead and a pulse travels instantly to that one person. Their matching bead lights and vibrates. No reply needed. No pressure.

Glow

Swipe to choose a presence color and quietly light their bead. A glance instead of a text — “you’re on my mind” without a single word.

The hardware ladder — every rung climbed

  • H1BLE link & app contract running on real nRF52840 silicon — full audio round-trip, zero dropped packets.
  • H2Five-wire bead bus: the hub drives the beads and reads their touches, every frame CRC-checked.
  • H3On-board mic records a real ~7-second voice charm and streams it over Bluetooth.
  • H4That charm plays back cleanly through the speaker amp — BLE, bus, mic & audio all live at once.
  • H5Battery sense over the air, plus deep-sleep power so it idles for weeks, not hours.
  • H6The whole loop, end to end, on one bench: tap → glow → record → hear → reply.

Proven on the core hub with emulated friend beads (June 2026). The first friend-bead PCBs are in fabrication now — putting them in the bracelet is the next rung.

Designed, coded and routed end-to-end by Melvin Osei Opoku — a neuroscience and signal-processing background with prior medical-device PCB work — building at the University of Florida.

Questions

When can I get one?

We’re hand-building the first units now. Join the circle and you’ll be first to hear ship dates — before any public announcement.

How much will it cost?

Founding-circle pricing is being finalized. Circle members lock it in first, ahead of the public price.

Do my friends need to buy one too?

No. You buy one bracelet and invite people from the app — they can light and speak to your beads from their phone before they ever own hardware. When they get a bracelet, it becomes two-way.

What actually works today?

The full loop runs on our hardware bench right now: record a voice charm, send it, feel it land with light and haptics, replay it. The first friend beads are in fabrication. See the proof.

Is it a smartwatch or fitness tracker?

No. M’AKOMA is jewelry first — no screens, no feeds, no step counts, no notifications. Just the few people you choose, with you all the time.

How is it different from Bond Touch?

Bond Touch proved people want a physical signal, but it’s a single tap between two people. M’AKOMA carries light, haptics and your real voice, for up to eight chosen people — each on a bead with its own Adinkra meaning.

Can I give one as a gift?
